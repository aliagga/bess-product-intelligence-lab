# Demo Script — BESS Product Intelligence Lab V1

## Demo Length

Target duration: 4–5 minutes

## Demo Objective

The objective of this demo is to show how BESS Product Intelligence Lab V1 connects battery-storage market logic, product KPIs, grid-constraint explainability, and roadmap prioritization into one product-management prototype.

This is not a production trading platform. It is a compact product sprint designed to demonstrate how a Senior Product Manager can translate energy-market complexity into product decisions, customer value, and roadmap priorities.

---

# 1. Opening — Why I Built This

Approximate time: 30 seconds

This project was built as a focused product sprint around grid-scale battery storage software.

The main idea is simple: battery owners, investors, developers, and internal teams do not only need optimized dispatch. They also need to understand where revenue comes from, why value is lost, which constraints affect performance, and which product features should be prioritized next.

So the project combines four layers:

1. BESS revenue scenario logic
2. Market and grid-constraint signals
3. Product and customer KPIs
4. Roadmap prioritization

The goal is to show product thinking, technical feasibility, and roadmap ownership.

---

# 2. Product Problem

Approximate time: 45 seconds

The core product problem is that battery storage value is complex.

A battery can create value through Day-Ahead arbitrage, FCR, aFRR, hybrid participation, and future markets such as intraday or imbalance management.

But customers often need answers to product-level questions:

* Which market created my revenue?
* Why did my asset underperform?
* How much value was lost because of grid constraints?
* Which strategy is better for my asset?
* Which product feature would improve trust or reduce friction?

This prototype is designed to translate those technical signals into product decisions.

---

# 3. Dashboard Walkthrough — Executive Overview

Approximate time: 45 seconds

The Executive Overview gives a quick product-level summary.

It shows:

* Number of sample BESS assets
* Number of revenue scenarios
* Best scenario revenue
* Average customer health score
* Missed revenue due to grid constraints
* Top roadmap priority

This section is designed for a product leader, CPO, sales lead, or customer-success user who needs a fast understanding of where value and product opportunities are located.

---

# 4. Market Intelligence

Approximate time: 45 seconds

The Market Intelligence section shows the synthetic market signals used by V1.

It includes:

* Day-Ahead price signal
* FCR price signal
* aFRR up and down price signals
* Grid constraint score
* Imbalance risk score

The product point is that these signals are not only technical inputs. They help explain customer value, arbitrage opportunities, reserve-market value, risk, and constraint-driven missed revenue.

In V2, this section would be connected to real market data.

---

# 5. Revenue Scenario Comparison

Approximate time: 60 seconds

The Revenue Scenario Comparison section compares six battery operation strategies:

1. Day-Ahead only
2. FCR only
3. aFRR only
4. Day-Ahead + FCR
5. Day-Ahead + FCR + aFRR
6. Grid-constrained hybrid operation

The goal is to show how a product can help customers compare strategies and understand why hybrid participation may create more value than a single-market strategy.

The market-share view also supports revenue attribution, which is essential for customer trust, investor communication, and sales enablement.

---

# 6. Asset Product KPIs

Approximate time: 60 seconds

The Asset Product KPIs section connects commercial performance with product-management signals.

It includes:

* Best scenario
* Revenue uplift versus Day-Ahead baseline
* Customer health score
* Product opportunity score
* Primary product need

This is where the project moves beyond a technical model.

The product opportunity score helps identify which asset or customer needs product attention, such as better reporting, grid-constraint explainability, market strategy comparison, or operational alerting.

---

# 7. Grid Constraint Impact

Approximate time: 45 seconds

The Grid Constraint Impact section explains how local or grid limitations can reduce available battery capability and create missed revenue.

The important product point is that missed revenue should not remain hidden in technical outputs. It should be translated into customer-facing explanations.

This directly supports product features such as:

* Grid Constraint Explainability
* Missed Revenue Explanation
* Operational Alert Intelligence
* Monthly Customer Report

---

# 8. Roadmap Prioritization

Approximate time: 60 seconds

The Roadmap Prioritization section uses RICE-style scoring.

The prototype prioritizes features such as:

* Revenue Attribution Engine
* Grid Constraint Explainability
* Market Strategy Scenario Explorer
* Customer Monthly Report Generator
* Operational Alert Intelligence
* Investor Risk Dashboard
* Co-location View

The roadmap is not based on random feature ideas. It is connected to evidence from revenue scenarios, grid constraints, product usage, customer health, support tickets, and manual overrides.

This shows how product decisions can be connected to customer and business value.

---

# 9. V2 Roadmap

Approximate time: 45 seconds

V1 is intentionally compact and sendable.

V2 is designed to increase realism and product maturity through:

* Real market data integration
* Optimization-based BESS dispatch
* Battery degradation-aware operation
* Forecasting and uncertainty
* Intraday and imbalance logic
* Co-located PV + BESS use case
* Automated customer reporting
* Investor revenue-risk dashboard
* API-ready architecture

The V1 scope demonstrates product clarity and fast execution. The V2 roadmap shows how the same concept can evolve into a more advanced product-intelligence layer.

---

# 10. Closing

Approximate time: 30 seconds

The main message of this project is that battery optimization products should not only calculate value. They should explain value.

This V1 prototype demonstrates how market data, battery scenario logic, product KPIs, customer signals, and roadmap prioritization can be combined into a product-management workflow.

It shows how I approach product work at the intersection of energy markets, battery storage, optimization, software, customer value, and strategic roadmap decisions.
