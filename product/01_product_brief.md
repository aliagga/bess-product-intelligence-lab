# Product Brief: BESS Product Intelligence Lab

## Product Sprint Version

V1 — Product-oriented MVP prototype

---

## Product Concept

BESS Product Intelligence Lab is a compact product prototype designed to show how battery storage market intelligence, revenue scenario analysis, product KPIs, and roadmap prioritization can support grid-scale BESS software decisions.

The concept is not to replicate a production battery trading platform. The purpose is to demonstrate how a Senior Product Manager can translate energy-market complexity into product requirements, customer value, software features, measurable KPIs, and roadmap priorities.

---

## Problem Statement

Grid-scale battery storage owners, developers, investors, and operators need more than optimized dispatch. They also need to understand:

* Where battery revenues come from
* Which market strategies create the most value
* How much value is lost due to grid constraints
* Why revenues differ between market strategies
* How operational friction affects customer trust
* Which product features should be prioritized based on customer and business value

Without this product-intelligence layer, customers may see revenue outputs but not understand the underlying drivers, limitations, risks, or improvement opportunities.

## Customer Pain Points

### 1. Revenue uncertainty

Battery asset owners and investors need to understand whether revenue is coming from Day-Ahead arbitrage, FCR, aFRR, hybrid market participation, or other future value pools.

### 2. Lack of explainability

Customers often need a clear explanation of why an asset performed well or poorly, especially when grid constraints, market volatility, or operational limitations affect results.

### 3. Difficulty comparing strategies

A battery can participate in multiple markets, but customers need simple views comparing DA-only, reserve-only, hybrid, and constrained strategies.

### 4. Grid-constraint impact

Grid constraints can reduce usable power and lead to missed revenue. This needs to be translated into customer-facing explanations and product features.

### 5. Product and operational friction

Support tickets, manual overrides, low engagement, and poor customer understanding can indicate that the product needs better reporting, alerts, and transparency.

## Product Opportunity

There is an opportunity to create a product-intelligence layer that connects:

* BESS revenue scenarios
* Market strategy comparison
* Grid-constraint explainability
* Product usage data
* Customer health indicators
* Roadmap prioritization

This layer helps internal and external users understand not only what happened, but why it happened and what product action should follow.

---

## V1 Scope

The V1 prototype includes:

1. Synthetic market data
2. Sample BESS asset portfolio
3. Product usage and customer health data
4. Revenue scenario comparison
5. Market intelligence metrics
6. Asset-level product KPIs
7. Grid-constraint impact analysis
8. Roadmap prioritization using RICE-style scoring
9. Streamlit dashboard
10. Product documentation
11. V2 roadmap

---

## V1 Revenue Scenarios

The prototype compares:

1. Day-Ahead only
2. FCR only
3. aFRR only
4. Day-Ahead + FCR
5. Day-Ahead + FCR + aFRR
6. Grid-constrained hybrid operation

These scenarios are intentionally simplified. The goal is to show product thinking and technical feasibility, not to deliver production-grade trading optimization.

---

## Core Product Questions

The V1 prototype is designed to answer:

1. Which strategy creates the highest value for each BESS asset?
2. Which market contributes most to revenue?
3. How much value is lost because of grid constraints?
4. Which customer or asset has the highest product opportunity?
5. Which feature should be prioritized next?
6. Which product KPIs should be tracked?
7. How can technical results become customer-facing explanations?

## Value Proposition

BESS Product Intelligence Lab helps translate battery-market complexity into product decisions.

It supports:

* Better customer trust
* Clearer revenue explanation
* Faster sales and customer-success conversations
* Stronger roadmap prioritization
* Better alignment between product, engineering, trading, operations, and commercial teams

---

## MVP Success Metrics

V1 success is measured by whether the prototype can clearly demonstrate:

* Revenue comparison across multiple BESS strategies
* Revenue attribution across Day-Ahead, FCR, and aFRR
* Missed revenue due to grid constraints
* Asset-level product opportunity scoring
* Customer/product KPI logic
* Roadmap prioritization based on product evidence
* A credible V2 roadmap for deeper technical realism

---

## Out of Scope for V1

The following are intentionally excluded from V1:

* Production-grade trading execution
* Real-time market bidding
* Full optimization-based dispatch
* Battery degradation modeling
* Real market data integration
* Intraday and imbalance market modeling
* Forecasting and uncertainty modeling
* API deployment
* Real customer data

These are planned as part of the V2 roadmap.

## V1 Positioning

V1 is a focused, sendable product sprint.

It is designed to demonstrate speed, ownership, product clarity, technical feasibility, and roadmap thinking.

V2 will extend the concept with deeper market realism, optimization, degradation-aware operation, forecasting, co-location, investor reporting, and customer-facing automation.
